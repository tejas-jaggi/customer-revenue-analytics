# Phase 5 — Cross-Section Executive Insights (Running Tracker)

**Purpose.** A running list of the single most decision-relevant finding from each analytical section, captured *as each section completes* so the eventual Phase 5 Executive Synthesis can be assembled from a maintained record rather than rediscovered at the end. This is a tracker, **not** the synthesis — no cross-section narrative is drawn here yet; that is written only after Section G, on request.

**How to read.** Each entry is one headline insight plus the cross-section threads it opens or closes. Threads are resolved as later sections answer them.

## Section A — Executive KPI Summary
**Insight:** Returns are a larger revenue leak than discounts — returns erase $412.9K (18.8% of transacted value) versus 6.86% given up to discounts.
**Opens thread →** Returns are the higher-value operational target (pursued in C, G).

## Section B — Revenue Analysis
**Insight:** Growth has decelerated (138% → 38% YoY), and the deceleration is concentrated in the *holiday peak*, not underlying demand — December same-month YoY collapsed 129% → 8.8% while off-peak July still grew ~33%.
**Opens thread →** Why did the holiday engine plateau? (promotion/inventory/channel — candidate for Section E marketing timing, and Phase 7+ recommendations.)

## Section C — Product Performance Analysis
**Insight:** Revenue rank ≠ value rank. **Accessories** creates the most value (highest margin 70.5%, lowest returns 8.6%, #1 profit) despite ranking #2 in revenue; **Footwear** is #3 in revenue but *last* in returns-adjusted profit (54.2% margin + 27.8% returns compounding).
**Opens threads →** Why does Footwear return at 27.8%? (Section G reason codes.) Do high-value customers over-index on Accessories? (Section F.) Which channels bring Accessories vs Footwear buyers? (Section E.)

## Section D — Geographic Performance Analysis
**Insight:** Geography is a *weak* performance differentiator — regional revenue-per-customer index spans only 94–106. The South drives revenue by volume (biggest, average value); the West is best-balanced (high revenue + high value); the Northeast is a small high-value niche; the Midwest modestly trails on both. Strategic attention is better spent on product and customer dimensions, where spreads are far wider.
**Opens threads →** Does the West's edge come from acquisition-channel mix? (Section E.) Are high-value personas geographically concentrated or uniform? (Section F.) Do regional return rates vary enough to shift the narrow RPC gaps? (Section G.)

## Section E — Marketing Performance & Acquisition Quality
**Insight:** Paid acquisition brings the *highest-value* customers, overturning the usual "paid buys volume, owned buys loyalty" assumption — Paid Social leads on both volume (2,519 customers) and value ($294/customer), while Email/SMS and Organic/SEO are the lowest-value channels. Two inherited questions resolved negatively: acquisition channel explains **neither** the West's regional edge (channel mix is uniform across regions) **nor** category affinity (every channel sends ~31% to Accessories, ~14% to Footwear). The holiday-plateau question was **declined** — a lifetime attribute cannot explain a per-period timing phenomenon.
**Resolves →** West's edge and category affinity both traced *past* channel to customer composition (→ F). **Opens →** do high-value paid channels bring higher-return customers, partially offsetting their gross-revenue advantage? (→ G).

## Section F — Customer Value & Retention Analysis
**Insight:** Customer value is a **frequency** phenomenon, not a basket-size one — repeat customers place 7.52 orders vs 1.0 for one-timers (7.5×) at nearly identical AOV, so the 82.4% concentration is driven by *coming back*, not spending more per visit. The concentration is even sharper than the headline: the **Loyal 7+ segment is 17% of customers but 63% of net revenue** ($824 avg CLV), and the **top revenue decile alone drives 50.1%**. Retention, not upsell, is where value is created.
**Resolves →** West's edge is NOT customer composition (high-value customers uniform across regions 8.8–10.5%) — thread closed negatively across D+E+F. High-value customers do NOT over-index Accessories (value is not category-driven). Paid Social's value edge IS partly composition (11.4% high-value share vs Email/SMS 6.6%). **Declined:** Affiliate's repeat driver (persona unstored, ED-009).
**Carries to G →** do high-value/Loyal customers return at higher/lower rates (customer-level returns-adjusted value)? Does the High-Return persona surface as a behavioral cluster despite being unlabeled?

## Section G — Returns & Value Leakage Analysis
**Insight:** 74.5% of the $412.9K returns leak is **controllable** (Wrong Size alone 40.6%), making the largest operational drain also the most fixable. Footwear's 27.8% rate is a sizing problem (54.8% Wrong Size); Womenswear is the bigger *dollar* risk ($171K, 2× Footwear). Loyalty is **not** a return risk — Loyal customers return at the blended 17.1% and the top decile loses only 15% of value to returns (vs 22.3% for the rest), so high value survives returns intact. Returns are flat across regions and channels, so they don't disturb D or E — Paid Social's value edge survives. **G.7 leakage ranking:** controllable returns $307.7K (53.5%) > discounts $161.8K > non-controllable returns $105.2K; the modeled one-time retention opportunity (~$2.7M) dwarfs all realized leaks ~4×.
**Resolves →** every remaining Open thread (Footwear cause = sizing; Womenswear risk = dollar exposure; regional/channel returns = uniform; high-value/Loyal returns = not elevated; high-return cluster = detected behaviorally). Only the persona *label* stays deferred to Phase 6.
